Executive Summary
How to Read the Comp Universe
  CRA is best understood as an asset-backed recurring service platform: it owns or finances customer-embedded equipment, bundles service and maintenance, and collects recurring fees rather than selling the asset upfront.
Comp set What the customer pays for Why it helps explain CRA Main limitation
Home-comfort equipment rental Water heaters, HVAC, water treatment and related home-comfort assets under recurring rental / service plans. Closest operating overlap: equipment owned by provider, installed at the customer site, with service bundled into monthly fees. Local regulation, consumer cancellation practices, installer labor and provincial market structure can dominate economics.
Submetering / meter-asset management Meter assets plus metering, billing, monitoring, energy data and compliance support. Strong asset-backed revenue model with long-lived devices and regulatory / energy-efficiency demand. Assets are smaller and more standardized; value is more data / software heavy than mechanical equipment rental.
Point-of-use water Installed coolers, filtration, ice, sparkling water or coffee equipment plus filter changes and service. Customer-site equipment, recurring monthly service, route density, maintenance and consumables all matter. Lower-cost assets are easier to redeploy; revenue can include consumables and bottled-water routes.
Security / alarm monitoring Monitoring, service, access control, alarms, cameras and related installed equipment. Useful for recurring monthly revenue, churn, pricing, customer lifetime value and contract analytics. Value sits more in the monitoring contract, software, response network and brand than in owned mechanical equipment.
Transportation solutions Fleet, ground-support, rail or logistics equipment embedded in customer operations, often with maintenance included. Useful for understanding mission-critical equipment leasing, availability and redeployment across customers. Assets are larger, mobile and residual-value sensitive; demand can track airport, rail, freight or logistics volumes.
 
Recommended framing
Put the most weight on home-comfort equipment rental, then submetering / meter-asset management and point-of-use water. Use security to understand RMR, churn and lifetime value. Use transportation as a fleet-asset analogy, not as a direct residential equipment comp.
