Comparable Sector Assessment

  • Home comfort: Highly comparable installed-equipment model featuring long customer tenures, recurring rental payments, maintenance responsibility, meaningful switching friction and attractive margins, with differences primarily in residential exposure and market-specific contract structures.
  • Submetering: Fairly comparable recurring-revenue model built around embedded onsite assets, long-duration contracts, low service intensity and strong retention, although regulatory support, mandated adoption and technology exposure can enhance defensibility.
  • Point-of-use water: Fairly comparable equipment-as-a-service model with installed customer assets, recurring payments, maintenance and route-density benefits, but shorter tenures, office exposure, consumables and service frequency reduce comparability.
  • Environmental solutions: Useful reference for essential, recurring environmental services, resilient demand and consolidation potential, but economics are often driven by routes, labor, fuel, permits, treatment assets and waste volumes.
  • Transportation solutions: Provides useful parallels in mission-critical equipment ownership, long-term customer relationships and full-service maintenance, yet mobile fleets, utilization, residual values, aviation or rail cyclicality and heavier capex limit comparability.
  • Specialty rental: Structurally less comparable because value depends on short-term demand, fleet utilization, project cycles, redeployment and higher churn; nevertheless, shared asset ownership and maintenance requirements establish a credible valuation floor.
