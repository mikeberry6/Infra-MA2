Weekly Briefing • March 7 – March 13, 2026
KEY THEMES
UK regulated utility ownership reshuffles as EQT Infrastructure and GIC acquire Kelda Holdings (Yorkshire Water) from DWS Infrastructure

Sponsors expanding the M&A playbook beyond traditional buyouts: distressed Section 363 carve-outs (ECP / TPI Composites), structured preferred equity for IRA tax credit monetization (Goldman Sachs / Nighthawk), and simultaneous platform + bolt-on integration (Ridgewood / Sierra Railroad + CVAT)
Power & ET 9 Deals
Invenergy AMPCI Thermal Power (IATP) | ArcLight Capital
ArcLight Capital (Stake Acquisition) · Thermal Power · US
ArcLight Capital Partners signed a definitive agreement to acquire InfraBridge’s (DigitalBridge) 50% stake in Invenergy AMPCI Thermal Power, a diversified portfolio of 11 power infrastructure assets totaling ~5.4 GW of generation capacity across seven North American markets. Invenergy will retain its existing ownership interest and continue managing operations of the portfolio, which includes highly efficient combined-cycle facilities such as Grays Harbor (WA), Nelson (IL), Lackawanna (PA), and St. Clair (ON). BofA Securities advised InfraBridge; Morgan Stanley advised ArcLight.

Gate City Power / Gate City Renewable Fuels / JET | Ara Partners
Ara Partners (Platform Acquisition) · Power & Fuels · US
Ara Energy, a portfolio platform of Ara Partners, agreed to acquire Gate City Power, Gate City Renewable Fuels, and an interest in the JET retail network from HF Capital for ~$875mm. Gate City Power owns thermal generation assets totaling ~2.2 GW across NYISO and ISO-NE, while Gate City Renewable Fuels comprises U.S. ethanol production assets; the JET retail network will be acquired in partnership with Ara Infrastructure.
Source
 
TAKKION | Apollo
Apollo (Divestiture Exit) · Infrastructure Services · US
Siris agreed to acquire a majority stake in TAKKION from funds managed by Apollo Global Management. TAKKION, founded in 2019 and headquartered in Centennial, Colorado, is the premier integrated logistics, O&M, and technical services provider to the renewable energy industry, comprising Transportation Partners & Logistics, Global Specialized Services, RENEW Energy, and Airway Services (~$600mm revenue in 2025). Siris plans to expand TAKKION’s service offerings across wind, solar, battery, and transmission sectors.
Source
 
Steel River Solar and Storage Project | EQT Infrastructure
EQT Infrastructure (Project Acquisition) · Solar & Storage · US
Cypress Creek Renewables, a portfolio company of EQT Infrastructure, acquired the Steel River solar and storage project in Northeast Arkansas from Swift Current Energy (backed by IFM Investors). The facility combines 2,450 MWdc (1,500 MWac) of solar generation with 2,900 MWh (720 MW) of battery storage across three equal phases, making it one of the largest solar and energy storage projects in the United States. The project is expected to be placed in service in 2029.
Source
 
Nighthawk Energy Storage Project | Goldman Sachs AM
Goldman Sachs AM (Preferred Equity) · BESS · US
Goldman Sachs Alternatives committed a $169mm preferred equity investment in Arevon Energy’s 300 MW / 1,200 MWh Nighthawk Energy Storage Project in Poway, California, as part of a broader $920mm financing package. The total financing comprises a $482mm debt facility led by CIBC, the Goldman Sachs preferred equity tranche structured to simplify tax credit monetization, and a $268mm tax credit transfer commitment. The lithium iron phosphate battery facility will provide resource adequacy capacity to PG&E under a long-term contract and is expected to become operational later in 2026.
Source
 
TPI Composites (Wind Blade Manufacturing) | Energy Capital Partners
Energy Capital Partners (Carve-Out) · Wind Manufacturing · US
Energy Capital Partners, through ECP Blade Holdings, agreed to acquire TPI Composites’ global wind blade manufacturing and services operations via a Section 363 bankruptcy sale for $20mm in cash plus assumed liabilities. TPI, the only independent wind blade manufacturer with a global footprint, filed for Chapter 11 and secured $82.5mm in DIP financing as it navigates the restructuring process. The transaction is subject to bankruptcy court approval, Oaktree lender consent, and regulatory approvals, with a drop-dead date of June 30, 2026.
Source
 
70.4 MWp Brandenburg Solar Park | Nuveen Infrastructure
Nuveen Infrastructure (Asset Acquisition) · Solar · Germany
Nuveen’s European Core Renewable Infrastructure Strategy fund acquired a 70.4 MWp solar park in Brandenburg, Germany from Trianel Energieprojekte, a subsidiary of municipal utility cooperative Trianel GmbH. The solar park, located along the Berlin-Halle railway line, was commissioned in April 2025 and meets EU Taxonomy requirements. Trianel, backed by 53 municipal shareholders, develops wind, solar, and battery storage projects across Germany and Austria.
Source
 
Maaselänkangas Onshore Wind Farm | DWS Infrastructure
DWS Infrastructure (Asset Acquisition) · Wind · Finland
DWS Group acquired the Maaselänkangas onshore wind farm in Oulainen, Finland from wpd Group on behalf of its European infrastructure investment strategy. The facility comprises seven Nordex turbines with 41.3 MW of installed capacity, has been operational since September 2024, and is expected to generate ~128.6 GWh of annual net energy output. The acquisition underscores DWS’s systematic expansion into European renewable energy assets.
Source
 
Dandelion Partnership | CVC DIF / Diverso Energy
CVC DIF / Diverso Energy (Joint Venture) · Geothermal · US
Diverso Energy, majority-owned by CVC DIF (~€23bn infrastructure AUM), announced a strategic partnership with Dandelion Energy to accelerate geothermal heat pump deployment across new U.S. residential housing developments. Under a “Geo-as-a-Service” model, Diverso and Dandelion will finance, install, and own geothermal systems, providing homebuilders with a zero-upfront-cost turnkey solution. The partnership targets three to four initial U.S. states, addressing a market where only ~1% of U.S. homes currently have geothermal heat pumps.
Source
Digital 4 Deals
GFiber / Astound Broadband | Stonepeak
Stonepeak (Platform Combination) · Fiber/Broadband · US
Stonepeak and Alphabet announced the combination of GFiber (formerly Google Fiber) with Stonepeak’s Astound Broadband, creating a leading independent fiber broadband provider with a combined footprint of ~7.1mm locations across the U.S.. Stonepeak will hold majority ownership of the combined entity, with Alphabet retaining a significant minority stake; the combined platform will be led by GFiber’s existing executive team. The transaction enables GFiber to operate independently while pairing its high-growth metropolitan fiber networks with Astound’s established infrastructure and customer base.
Source
 
Lefdal Mine Datacenter | 3i Infrastructure
3i Infrastructure (Majority Stake) · Data Centers · Norway
3i Infrastructure agreed to invest ~€300mm to acquire a majority stake in Lefdal Mine Datacenter, a Norwegian data center campus located in a former mine with 37 MW of operational capacity and 43 MW under construction. The site leverages Norway’s low-cost, reliable power supply and a closed-loop seawater cooling system for high energy efficiency, with significant expansion potential across five undeveloped mine levels. The total investment, including growth capital, is expected to reach ~€400mm.
Source
 
Advanced Cooling Technologies (ACT) | Blackstone
Blackstone (Majority Stake) · Data Center Cooling · US
Blackstone Energy Transition Partners agreed to acquire a majority stake in Advanced Cooling Technologies, a Pennsylvania-based manufacturer of highly-engineered thermal management and energy efficiency solutions serving data center, defense, space, and energy applications. ACT’s product suite, including two-phase liquid cooling, heat pipes, and cold plates, is well-positioned to benefit from rising power density and cooling requirements driven by AI infrastructure buildout. ACT’s executive team will remain in place and continue as significant shareholders.
Source
 
Arqiva | IFM Investors
IFM Investors (Divestiture) · Broadcast Infra · UK
Funds managed by IFM Investors agreed to divest their 14.84% minority stake in Arqiva, the UK’s leading broadcast and telecommunications infrastructure operator, to Polus Capital Management for GBP8.9mm. The sale follows Polus’s earlier acquisition of Macquarie Asset Management’s 26.54% stake, bringing Polus’s combined equity interest in Arqiva to 41.38%. Arqiva’s revenue base is underpinned by long-term, RPI-linked contracts with investment-grade counterparties including the BBC and major mobile network operators.
Source
Transportation 2 Deals
Sierra Railroad Company | Ridgewood Infrastructure
Ridgewood Infrastructure (Platform Acquisition) · Rail · US
Ridgewood Infrastructure acquired a controlling interest in Sierra Railroad Company, a California-based shortline rail platform providing freight rail, switching, storage, and transloading services across ~130 miles of track. Founded in 1897, Sierra’s network is strategically positioned near major agricultural and dairy production regions, key West Coast ports, and major industrial centers, with interconnections to both Union Pacific and BNSF Class I networks. The platform also includes Railpower, Inc., which owns the only FRA-approved hydrogen-powered locomotive in the U.S.
Source
 
Central Valley Ag Transport (CVAT) | Ridgewood Infrastructure
Ridgewood Infrastructure (Bolt-On) · Rail · US
Simultaneously with Ridgewood’s acquisition of Sierra Railroad, Sierra acquired Central Valley Ag Transport, an agricultural products transload facility owner and operator serving customers along Sierra’s rail network. The bolt-on vertically integrates agricultural transload capabilities essential to California’s dairy and broader agricultural industry, enabling efficient movement of feed and agricultural products while deepening customer relationships and increasing rail utilization.
Source
Utilities 1 Deal
Kelda Holdings (Yorkshire Water) | EQT Infrastructure & GIC
EQT Infrastructure & GIC (Minority Stake) · Water · UK
EQT Active Core Infrastructure agreed to acquire a 42% shareholding in Kelda Holdings, the parent company of Yorkshire Water, which provides essential water and wastewater services to ~5.5mm customers across over two million homes and 140,000 businesses in the UK. The transaction sees DWS Infrastructure and Corsair Capital exit their minority positions while GIC increases its stake to 42% and TCorp to 16%, positioning the new shareholder group to support an GBP8.3bn investment program between 2025 and 2030. EQT has also committed to contributing further equity to strengthen the balance sheet.
Source
Midstream 1 Deal
Gorgon LNG / Ichthys LNG | EIG / MidOcean Energy
EIG / MidOcean Energy (Minority Stake) · LNG · Australia
MidOcean Energy, a globally diversified LNG platform formed and managed by EIG Global Energy Partners, entered into definitive agreements to acquire JERA’s 0.417% interest in the Chevron-operated 15.6 mtpa Gorgon LNG project, increasing MidOcean’s stake to 1.417%. The transaction perimeter also includes JERA’s 0.735% interest in the Inpex-operated 8.9 mtpa Ichthys LNG project, which will be transferred to an existing Ichthys JV participant upon closing. UBS served as financial advisor and White & Case as legal advisor to MidOcean.
