Weekly Briefing – Infrastructure Sponsor M&A – 12 deals across Transportation, Social, Power & ET, Digital, and Midstream – Mar 28–Apr 3, 2026
GUGGENHEIM
INFRASTRUCTURE COVERAGE & ADVISORY
Infrastructure Sponsor M&A Activity
Weekly Briefing • March 28 – April 3, 2026
KEY THEMES
A 12-deal week across six sectors produced no large-cap transactions, with Nxtra’s US$1.0bn funding round the closest to a headline size.
Four of the twelve deals targeted US assets, with Equitix / John Laing, Igneo, Harrison Street, and Antin each deploying capital across road PPP, cold storage logistics, outpatient healthcare, and CNG/LNG infrastructure, respectively.
Transportation 4 Deals
Mattingly Cold Storage | Igneo Infrastructure Partners
ChillCo Logistics / Igneo Infrastructure Partners (Platform Acquisition) · Cold Storage Logistics · US
Igneo agreed to acquire Mattingly Cold Storage, a 275k sq. ft. multi-temperature, post-production focused cold storage business in Zanesville, Ohio, as the foundation of ChillCo Logistics. The deal marks Igneo’s inaugural U.S. cold-storage investment and extends its transportation and logistics exposure.
Source
 
I-4 Ultimate Project (50% stake from Skanska) | Equitix / John Laing
Equitix / John Laing (Stake Acquisition) · Road PPP · US
John Laing Investments Limited, jointly owned by Equitix and John Laing, reached financial close on the acquisition of Skanska’s 50% stake in I-4 Ultimate, securing full ownership of the asset. The project covers approximately 21 miles of Interstate 4 in central Florida and serves more than 200,000 motorists daily under a 40-year availability-based P3 concession.
Source
 
Cross London Trains | InfraRed Capital Partners
InfraRed Capital Partners / HICL (Minority Stake Acquisition) · Rail Rolling Stock · UK
HICL agreed to acquire a further 6.65% interest in Cross London Trains for approximately £52mm, taking its total economic interest to 13.13%. XLT owns the fleet of 115 Siemens Class 700 electric trains operating on Thameslink, one of the UK’s most important transport corridors linking St Pancras, Gatwick Airport and Luton Airport.
Source
 
24H Frost | I Squared Capital
CubeCold / I Squared Capital (Bolt-On) · Cold Storage Logistics · France
CubeCold entered the French market through the acquisition of 24H Frost, adding a fully automated cold-storage facility in the port of Loon-Plage, Dunkirk. The site offers more than 12,000 frozen pallet positions, and the acquisition expands CubeCold’s network to over 700,000 pallet spaces across six European markets.
Source
Social 3 Deals
UK social infrastructure PPP portfolio | Equitix
Equitix (Portfolio Acquisition) · Social Infrastructure PPP · UK
Equitix completed the acquisition of 100% of the equity and subordinated debt interests in a UK social infrastructure PPP portfolio from Sir Robert McAlpine Capital Ventures for Equitix Fund VII. The assets include Gateshead Schools PPP, Redcar Primary Care Hospital PPP and an additional 50% interest in City of Glasgow College PPP, serving more than 6,500 students and providing more than 30 community hospital beds.
Source
 
Orbis Protect | Tiger Infrastructure Partners
Tiger Infrastructure Partners (Growth Capital Investment) · Security Infrastructure · UK
Tiger agreed a transformational growth capital investment in Orbis Protect, a UK-based owner and operator of dynamic security solutions. The capital is intended to fund further technology investment, expansion across the UK and Europe, and continued international growth, while facilitating Synova’s exit.
Source
 
Northside East Cobb Medical Center | Harrison Street
Harrison Street / Stonemont (Acquisition) · Outpatient Healthcare · US
Harrison Street partnered with Stonemont on the acquisition of Northside East Cobb Medical Center, a 103,184 sq. ft., 100%-leased medical office building in Marietta, Georgia. Anchored by Northside Hospital, the four-story facility houses imaging, orthopedics, primary care, urgent care, and physician specialty services.
Source
Power & ET 2 Deals
Mercia Power Response + Balance Power Projects | InfraVia Capital Partners
InfraVia Capital Partners (Platform Creation) · Flexible Power · UK
InfraVia acquired majority stakes in Mercia Power Response and Balance Power and will merge the two businesses into Supernova Power, a UK-focused flexible power platform. Mercia operates 262MW of gas peaking and battery storage assets with more than 400MW in development, while Balance adds a battery storage pipeline of over 1.4GW.
Source
 
Three 5MW biomass plants in Croatia | Ancala Partners
Croatian Biomass Platform / Ancala Partners (Bolt-On) · Biomass · Croatia
Ancala’s Croatian Biomass Platform acquired three 5MW biomass plants from The Sherif Group, more than doubling renewable generation capacity to 25MW. The five-site platform now generates approximately 200,000 MWh annually, equivalent to powering more than 45,000 homes, with the newly acquired plants backed by long-term feed-in tariffs and biomass supply agreements.
Source
Digital 2 Deals
Nxtra Data Limited | Carlyle
Carlyle / Airtel (Growth Equity / Minority Stake) · Data Centers · India
Carlyle participated in Airtel’s US$1.0bn funding round for Nxtra, which values the data-center platform at around US$3.1bn post-closing. Carlyle is committing US$240mm, and Nxtra plans to deploy the capital to expand its network across India, scaling from roughly 300MW today toward 1GW over time.
Source
 
Selected Data Center Assets from NEC Corporation | DigitalBridge
DigitalBridge / JEXI (Carve-Out / Acquisition) · Data Centers · Japan
DigitalBridge and JEXI completed the acquisition of selected NEC data-center assets in the Greater Tokyo and Kansai regions. The assets will operate as a new standalone platform, with NEC retained as anchor customer under contract as the business seeks to add third-party colocation demand.
Source
Midstream 1 Deal
Sapphire Gas Solutions | Antin Infrastructure Partners / Apollo Global Management
Antin Infrastructure Partners / Apollo (Platform Buyout / Divestiture) · CNG / LNG Infrastructure · US
Antin, through Flagship Fund V, acquired Sapphire Gas Solutions from Apollo-managed funds. Sapphire owns and operates infrastructure to compress, liquefy, transport and store CNG and LNG, operates across 30 U.S. states, and serves more than 120 customers.
