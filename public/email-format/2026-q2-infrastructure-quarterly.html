Executive Summary
Infrastructure activity remained robust through Q1, led by power and renewables as investors pursued scaled generation, grid reliability, and digital-load exposure. Against a resilient but more volatile macro backdrop, fundraising stayed constructive while financing became more selective and asset quality increasingly determined execution certainty.

01  Deal Activity Monitor
Infrastructure deal activity started the year strongly, with global announced M&A exceeding $110bn across more than 100 transactions. Power and renewables led as investors favored scaled generation platforms and portfolios tied to grid reliability, decarbonization, and rising digital load.
Headline transactions included the $33.4bn sale of AES Corporation, the $6.5bn sale of Boralex, and gas-generation portfolios involving Constellation Energy ($5.0bn), Cogentrix Energy ($4.0bn), and ECP ($3.5bn). Transport supplied a second pocket of scale through Qube ($8.3bn), Macquarie AirFinance ($7.0bn), United Ports ($2.4bn), and TCR Group ($1.3bn).

02  Macro Spotlight
Headline risk has returned, but the bar for a material slowdown remains high.
Early-2026 fundamentals remain constructive, supported by resilient U.S. activity and generally favorable financial conditions. Geopolitical escalation and the effective closure of the Strait of Hormuz nevertheless pushed oil prices more than 47% higher, reviving supply-chain and inflation concerns.
The economy enters that shock from a position of relative strength: February core inflation was 2.5% year over year, five-year forward inflation expectations remained near 2.0%, and labor and housing data stayed firm. Guggenheim Investments continues to expect positive 12-month growth, a Federal Reserve easing bias, and fiscal support for capital-intensive sectors.
Source: Guggenheim Investments, Quarterly Macro Themes, Q1 2026.
03  Sector Spotlight
Power deal momentum deepens as the sector dominates North American infrastructure M&A activity.
Q1 dealmaking concentrated sharply in power: eight of the nine North American infrastructure transactions with disclosed values above $500mm involved power and renewables. The pattern reflects growing urgency to secure firm, scalable generation for digital infrastructure and broader reliability needs.
The largest deals combined corporate platforms with sizeable gas-generation portfolios, signaling that investors are underwriting availability, dispatchability, and system value alongside decarbonization.

04  Product Spotlight
LP appetite has returned, but infrastructure fundraising is becoming a barbell market.
Infrastructure AUM exceeded $1.6tn after a record approximately $200bn fundraising year in 2025, reinforcing the asset class's resilience within private markets. LP allocations remain supported by demand for inflation protection, durable cash flows, and operational value creation.
Capital formation is increasingly concentrated: nearly three-quarters of new commitments flowed to the top 50 mega-funds, while approximately 70% targeted core-plus and value-add strategies. Open-ended vehicles now represent roughly 10% of the market and average hold periods have extended to 7.6 years.

Read-through: LP appetite remains intact, but capital formation is concentrating around managers that can pair scale with operational value creation and differentiated sourcing.
05  Financing Market
Financing remains available for infrastructure, but terms are increasingly sorting by asset quality, narrative risk, and sponsor credibility.
Leveraged credit markets entered 2026 with solid momentum before sentiment weakened in February amid AI disruption concerns, geopolitical escalation, persistent inflation, portfolio stress, and rising redemption requests. Primary syndicated issuance slowed, although higher-quality infrastructure credits continued to attract demand and CLO creation preserved meaningful dry powder.
Private-credit new-issue spreads widened an estimated 50-100 bps. High-quality sponsor-backed unitranche pricing moved from approximately S+450 entering the year to S+500 or wider, shifting lender attention toward portfolio management and placing greater value on asset quality, sponsor credibility, and process certainty.
