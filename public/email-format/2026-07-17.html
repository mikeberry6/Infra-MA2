GUGGENHEIM

Infrastructure Sponsor M&A Activity
Weekly Briefing • July 11 – July 17, 2026
KEY THEMES
Power & ET led with 6 of 13 deals, anchored by GIP-backed Aditya Birla Renewables’ US$1.8 billion agreement for Sprng Energy. GIP also agreed to acquire control of Summit Ridge Energy, J.P. Morgan Asset Management-backed Sonnedix added 260 MW / 1,040 MWh of Italian battery storage, and MEAG acquired a 49.78% interest in SELCHP. ClearGen’s operating solar portfolio and ConTeras’s CD Energy Services bolt-on rounded out the sector.
U.S. deployment centered on Summit Ridge Energy and the ClearGen solar portfolio, while I Squared-backed Liberty Tire Recycling, Igneo’s new medical-waste platform and Oaktree-backed ConTeras added portfolio-company acquisitions. MidOcean’s Delfin FLNG2 agreement extended activity into U.S. LNG infrastructure; Sprng, Tuscania BESS, SELCHP, EcoEridania, TIP Group and Raxio supplied the principal non-U.S. contrast.
Power & ET6 Deals
Sprng Energy | GIP
GIP / Aditya Birla Renewables (Portfolio Company Acquisition) · Renewable Power · India
GIP-backed Aditya Birla Renewables signed an agreement to acquire 100% of Solenergi, including the Sprng Energy group, from Shell for US$1.8 billion. The platform comprises 5.0 GWp of Indian solar and wind assets, including 3.3 GWp operating and 1.7 GWp contracted.
Source
Summit Ridge Energy | GIP
GIP (Majority Stake) · Commercial Solar & Storage · United States
GIP Mid-Market Funds agreed to acquire a majority and controlling interest in Summit Ridge Energy. The U.S. commercial solar and storage platform owns and operates more than 275 facilities serving over 60,000 businesses, homes and municipalities.
Source
Tuscania BESS Portfolio | J.P. Morgan Asset Management
J.P. Morgan Asset Management / Sonnedix (Portfolio Company Acquisition) · Battery Storage · Italy
J.P. Morgan Asset Management-backed Sonnedix acquired two adjacent standalone BESS projects in Tuscania from Sphera Energy. The 160 MW and 100 MW four-hour projects add 260 MW / 1,040 MWh to Sonnedix’s Italian portfolio.
Source
SELCHP | MEAG / iCON Infrastructure
MEAG / iCON Infrastructure (Minority Stake / Sale) · Waste-to-Energy · United Kingdom
MEAG acquired iCON Infrastructure Partners IV’s 49.78% stake in the South East London Combined Heat and Power plant. Veolia remains co-owner and operator of the facility, which receives contracted waste volumes and generates power-offtake revenue.
Source
ClearGen C&I Solar Portfolio | CBRE Investment Management
CBRE Investment Management / ClearGen (Portfolio Company Acquisition) · C&I Solar · United States
CBRE Investment Management-backed ClearGen acquired 100% of the equity interests in ten operating behind-the-meter solar projects from Tortoise Capital affiliates. The 19 MW portfolio spans California, Colorado, Florida, Massachusetts and New Jersey.
Source
CD Energy Services | Oaktree
Oaktree / ConTeras (Portfolio Company Acquisition) · Energy Infrastructure Services · United States
Oaktree Power Opportunities-backed ConTeras acquired CD Energy Services, a Denver-based specialty contractor serving customers across the Rocky Mountain region and western United States. The bolt-on expands ConTeras’s national industrial and energy-services footprint.
Source
Social Infra4 Deals
EcoEridania | CVC DIF / iCON Infrastructure
CVC DIF / iCON Infrastructure (Majority Stake / Sale) · Medical & Industrial Waste · Italy
CVC DIF agreed to acquire a majority stake in EcoEridania through DIF Infrastructure VIII, including the indirect purchase of iCON’s 75% interest. The integrated Italian waste platform served approximately 27,000 clients through 22 facilities and managed 1.3 million tons of waste in 2025.
Source
All American Tire / Colt Tire Recycling / Genan | I Squared Capital
I Squared Capital / Liberty Tire Recycling (Portfolio Company Acquisition) · Tire Recycling · United States
I Squared-backed Liberty Tire Recycling completed three acquisitions: All American Tire, Colt Tire Recycling and Genan Inc. The bolt-ons expand Liberty’s collection and recycling coverage across Gulf Coast markets and reinforce its environmental-infrastructure platform.
Source
Cyntox Biohazard Solutions | Igneo Infrastructure Partners
Igneo Infrastructure Partners (Buyout / Platform Formation) · Medical Waste · United States
Igneo acquired Cyntox Biohazard Solutions as one of two initial investments forming a regulated U.S. medical-waste platform. The combined platform will integrate collection, treatment and disposal services across the Central, Midwest and Northeast regions.
Source
AdvoWaste Medical Services | Igneo Infrastructure Partners
Igneo Infrastructure Partners (Buyout / Platform Formation) · Medical Waste · United States
Igneo separately acquired AdvoWaste Medical Services as the second founding business in its new North American medical-waste platform. AdvoWaste and Cyntox have complementary footprints and serve small and medium waste generators including clinics, veterinary practices and nursing homes.
Source
Digital1 Deal
Raxio Group | Meridiam
Meridiam / Roha (Follow-On Equity) · Data Centers · Africa
Meridiam and Roha provided additional equity to Raxio and increased their stakes, lifting the African data-center platform’s committed capital from US$350 million to more than US$380 million. Raxio operates Tier III carrier-neutral facilities in six countries with Tanzania under development.
Source
Transportation1 Deal
TIP Group | I Squared Capital / IMCO / GCM Grosvenor
I Squared Capital / IMCO / GCM Grosvenor (Minority Stake / Sale) · Transportation Equipment Leasing · Europe
I Squared agreed to sell a combined 25% stake in TIP Group to IMCO and GCM Grosvenor. TIP operates approximately 90,000 trailers, trucks and specialized assets from more than 130 service locations in 17 countries, serving over 15,000 European transportation and logistics customers.
Source
Midstream1 Deal
Delfin FLNG2 | EIG
EIG / MidOcean Energy (Minority Stake) · Floating LNG · United States
EIG-managed MidOcean Energy entered into agreements to acquire up to a 50% interest in Delfin’s second floating LNG vessel. The transaction remains subject to a positive final investment decision and customary conditions while advancing U.S. Gulf Coast LNG export capacity.
