Infrastructure Sponsor M&A Activity
Weekly Briefing • February 28 – March 6, 2026
KEY THEMES
A consortium led by GIP and EQT is executing the year’s largest infrastructure take-private by acquiring AES Corporation at an estimated $33.4bn enterprise value
Global Infrastructure Partners drove significant deal volume as both a buyer and a seller, acquiring TCR in a €2.5bn secondary buyout and fully exiting Naturgy Energy Group through a €2.79bn bookbuild
Investments in next-generation infrastructure are accelerating globally; new platforms across battery storage, wind energy, and data centers expanding throughout Europe and the Asia-Pacific region
Power & ET 8 Deals
AES Corporation | GIP & EQT Infrastructure
Take-Private · Diversified Power · US
A consortium led by Global Infrastructure Partners (a BlackRock subsidiary) and EQT Infrastructure VI agreed to acquire AES Corporation in an all-cash take-private at $15.00 per share (~$10.7bn equity value, ~$33.4bn EV), a 40.3% premium to the 30-day VWAP. AES operates ~34 GW of generation capacity across the Americas. CalPERS and Qatar Investment Authority are participating as co-underwriters.
Source
 
11 German BESS Projects | Allianz Global Investors
Equity Stake (50%) · BESS · Germany
TotalEnergies agreed to sell a 50% stake in 11 battery energy storage projects under construction in Germany to Allianz Global Investors, representing €500mm of total project investment. The portfolio has 789 MW / 1,628 MWh of capacity, developed by Kyon Energy (a TotalEnergies subsidiary). This marks AllianzGI’s first direct equity investment in utility-scale battery storage; all projects are expected to be operational by 2028.
Source
 
SK Eternix | KKR
Minority Stake (43.5%) · Renewables · South Korea
KKR agreed to acquire a combined 43.5% minority stake in SK Eternix from SK Discovery and Hahn & Company for KRW348bn (~$235mm). SK Eternix is a South Korean renewable energy platform spun off from SK D&D in 2024, operating across solar, onshore wind, energy storage systems, and fuel cell power generation. The company is Korea’s largest ESS operator and holds supply rights for Bloom Energy fuel cell equipment.
Source
 
50 MW Onshore Wind Project (Gia Lai) | Actis / Levanta Renewables
Add-On (80%) · Wind · Vietnam
Levanta Renewables, Actis’s pan-Southeast Asia renewable platform, acquired an 80% stake in a 50 MW operating onshore wind farm in Gia Lai Province, Vietnam, from Super Energy Corporation for $31.78mm. The asset generates >150 GWh annually under long-term PPAs with Vietnam Electricity, bringing Levanta’s total operating capacity to ~400 MW.
Source
 
Northview Energy | BCI, NBIM & Brookfield
Platform Launch · Renewables · North America
BCI, Norges Bank Investment Management, and Brookfield launched Northview Energy, a renewable energy platform to acquire contracted, operating assets in the U.S. and Canada. The seed portfolio comprises 22 sites (17 solar, 5 onshore wind) totaling ~2.3 GW across six U.S. power markets, with PPAs averaging 16 years remaining. The three investors will fund equally, with a $1.5bn framework for future acquisitions.
Source
 
Calisen | HarbourVest Partners
Secondary · Smart Metering · UK
HarbourVest Partners acquired a stake in Calisen, the UK’s leading independent smart metering company with an installed base of approximately 16mm meters, via a secondary transaction from Equitix. Calisen owns, finances, and installs smart metering infrastructure across the UK on behalf of energy suppliers. EQT and GIC hold the majority stake, acquired in 2024.
Source
 
79.2 MW DG BESS Portfolio | Bimergen Energy
Buyout · BESS · US
Bimergen Energy completed the acquisition of a portfolio of eight late-stage 9.9 MW distributed generation battery energy storage projects totaling 79.2 MW in ERCOT South from Aggreko’s IPP Solutions business (owned by I Squared Capital). The projects will provide frequency regulation, voltage support, energy arbitrage, and peak capacity support to the Texas grid, with commissioning expected from late 2026 through early 2027.
Source
 
Maaselänkangas Onshore Wind Farm | DWS Infrastructure
Buyout · Wind · Finland
DWS Group acquired the Maaselänkangas onshore wind farm, a 41.3 MW project comprising seven turbines located in Oulainen, Finland, from wpd Group. The acquisition was made on behalf of DWS’s European infrastructure investment strategy, which has been progressively building a portfolio of renewable energy assets across the continent.
Source
Digital 4 Deals
Gabia Data Centre | Macquarie Asset Management
JV Platform · Data Centers · South Korea
Macquarie Asset Management, through MAIF4, and Gabia Inc. announced a strategic partnership to develop a hyperscale data center platform in South Korea, with a total investment of ~KRW600bn (~$420mm) over four to six years targeting >100 MW of capacity. The first project is a 40 MW facility in Ansan, ~30 km south of Seoul. Gabia’s subsidiary KINX, South Korea’s only neutral internet exchange operator, will provide connectivity infrastructure.
Source
 
Arqiva | Polus Capital Management
Minority Stake (26.5%) · Broadcast Infra · UK
Polus Capital Management agreed to acquire a 26.5% shareholding in Arqiva from Macquarie Asset Management for GBP16.5mm, ending Macquarie’s 20-year investment in the UK broadcast and telecommunications infrastructure company. Arqiva operates the UK’s terrestrial television and radio transmission network and has a growing smart utilities networks business with an order book of approximately GBP2.8bn.
Source
 
Drogheda Energy Park | Schroders Greencoat
JV / Platform · Data Centers · Ireland
Greencoat Renewables and Schroders Greencoat’s SCSL Global Energy Infrastructure vehicle launched a 50:50 green digital infrastructure JV, with Drogheda Energy Park as its inaugural investment. The platform acquired the former Premier Periclase site in Drogheda, County Louth, at an advanced stage of planning consent for an initial 36 MW data centre combining on-site generation, storage, and grid services.
Source
 
Valokuitunen Oy | Brookfield Infrastructure
Equity Stake (51%) · Fiber/Broadband · Finland
CapMan Infra agreed to sell its 60% stake in Valokuitunen Oy, Finland’s largest independent FTTH operator, to Brookfield Infrastructure Structured Solutions (51%) and Telia (9%, increasing its ownership to 49%). The platform, established in 2020 as a Telia-CapMan JV, has built an open-access network reaching >400,000 homes.
Source
Transportation 2 Deals
TCR | Global Infrastructure Partners
Secondary Buyout (71%) · Ground Support · Europe
Global Infrastructure Partners (BlackRock) agreed to acquire TCR, the world’s largest independent lessor of airport ground support equipment, from 3i Infrastructure plc. TCR manages ~44,000 assets at >200 airports globally. 3i expects net proceeds of ~€1.14bn from its 71% stake, a 22% uplift to its September 2025 valuation.
Source
 
Gosport Ferry | Northleaf Capital / Thames Clippers
Bolt-On · Ferry · UK
TC Gosport Bidco, an indirect subsidiary of Uber Boat by Thames Clippers (backed by Northleaf Capital Partners), acquired the Portsmouth Harbour Ferry Company from FIH Group plc for GBP11.62mm. The ferry company operates the Gosport Ferry service, providing a pedestrian and cyclist link between Gosport and Portsmouth.
Source
Social 1 Deal
Aura Holdings | Stonepeak
Platform Equity · Retirement Villages · Australia
Stonepeak announced a platform equity investment in Aura Holdings, a leading retirement village developer in Queensland, Australia. The investment, together with committed development facilities, provides up to A$1bn to support Aura’s pipeline of >10 projects. This marks Stonepeak’s second platform commitment in the ANZ retirement village sector, following its acquisition of Arvida Group in November 2024.
Source
Utilities 1 Deal
Naturgy Energy Group | CriteriaCaixa
Block Trade (11.4%) · Gas & Power · Spain
BlackRock, through GIP, divested its remaining 11.4% stake in Naturgy Energy Group via an accelerated bookbuild, placing 110.8mm shares at €25.20 each (€2.79bn, ~5.8% discount to prior close). The sale completes GIP’s full exit from Naturgy, a position originally established in 2016.
