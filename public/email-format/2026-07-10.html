Weekly Briefing – Infrastructure Sponsor M&A – 16 deals across Power & ET, Social Infra, Digital, Transportation, Utilities, and Midstream – July 4–10, 2026
GUGGENHEIM
INFRASTRUCTURE COVERAGE & ADVISORY
Infrastructure Sponsor M&A Activity
Weekly Briefing • July 4 – July 10, 2026
KEY THEMES
Power & ET led with 6 of 16 deals. EQT Infrastructure signed for Copia Power, EIP-backed Alpiq agreed to acquire 90% of Harmony Energy, and Apollo agreed to exit its Great Bay Renewables interests for approximately US$390 million; EQT-backed Tion also added the 69 MWp Schwarzholz solar park. Across sectors, the US$1.13 billion investment in EIG’s MidOcean Energy was the largest disclosed deployment.
U.S. deployment centered on Copia Power, Sabey Data Center Properties, Milestone Environmental, the NuGen portfolio and Dresser Utility Solutions, while MidOcean added global LNG exposure through a North America-based platform. Apollo’s Great Bay transaction was an exit rather than new deployment; Harmony Energy, D-Marin, Helen’s charging network, Schwarzholz, Nicollin, Northern Fiber and the Kundl campus provided the principal European contrast.
Power & ET 6 Deals
Great Bay Renewables | Apollo
Apollo / Northampton Capital Partners (Sale) · Renewable Energy Royalties · United States
Apollo Infrastructure Funds signed an agreement to sell their 50% membership interests in Great Bay Renewables to Northampton Capital Partners for approximately US$390 million. The divestiture transfers Apollo's stake in the renewable-energy royalty platform.
Source
 
Copia Power | EQT Infrastructure / Carlyle Infrastructure
EQT Infrastructure / Carlyle Infrastructure (Buyout / Sale) · Integrated Power & AI Infrastructure · United States
EQT Infrastructure VII agreed to acquire Copia Power from Carlyle Infrastructure. The platform has 2.6 GW of generation and storage operating or under construction, with more than 9 GW of data-center development supported by over 25 GW of solar and storage and 7 GW of gas generation.
Source
 
Harmony Energy | Energy Infrastructure Partners
Energy Infrastructure Partners / Alpiq (Portfolio Company Acquisition) · Battery Storage · Europe
EIP-backed Alpiq agreed to acquire 90% of Harmony Energy, with co-founder and CEO Peter Kavanagh retaining 10%. The platform adds approximately 400 MW under construction and a pipeline exceeding 12 GW across the United Kingdom, France, Germany and Poland.
Source
 
Schwarzholz Agri-PV Park | EQT Infrastructure
EQT Infrastructure / Tion Renewables / FEFA (Portfolio Company Acquisition) · Solar / Agri-PV · Germany
EQT Active Core Infrastructure-backed Tion Renewables acquired the operational Schwarzholz Agri-PV park from FEFA. The 69 MWp asset is expected to generate approximately 87 GWh annually, equivalent to the electricity needs of about 25,000 homes.
Source
 
NuGen C&I Solar Portfolio | Argo Infrastructure Partners
Argo Infrastructure Partners / NuGen (Portfolio Acquisition) · Commercial & Industrial Solar · United States
Argo acquired eight operating commercial and industrial solar sites from NuGen, including six in Massachusetts and two in New Jersey. The add-on expands Argo's distributed-solar platform to 196 sites and approximately 270 MW of operating capacity.
Source
 
Lemvig Biogas | APG
APG / Palisade / BioticNRG (Portfolio Company Acquisition) · Biogas / Biomethane · Denmark
APG-backed BioticNRG acquired Lemvig Biogas, an industrial-scale Danish facility operating since 1992. BioticNRG plans to upgrade the plant to produce biomethane for injection into Denmark's gas grid.
Source
Social Infra 4 Deals
Nicollin Environnement | Morgan Stanley Infrastructure Partners
MSIP (Majority Stake) · Waste & Water Environmental Services · France
MSIP committed to acquire a majority stake in Nicollin Environnement, with the Nicollin family remaining invested. The French platform serves approximately 360 public contracts and 8,000 private clients with 4,800 employees, 2,900 vehicles and a national network of environmental-infrastructure sites.
Source
 
Paprec Metabader | Swiss Life Asset Managers / UBS Asset Management
Swiss Life Asset Managers / UBS Asset Management / Paprec (Business Combination) · Recycling & Environmental Services · Switzerland
Paprec Swiss Group combined with Metabader to create Paprec Metabader, which has 31 plants and agencies across Switzerland. Jessy Bader became CEO and a shareholder; the combined company’s capital includes an infrastructure fund managed by Swiss Life Asset Managers and UBS Asset Management.
Source
 
Kundl Campus Infrastructure & Site Services | Swiss Life Asset Managers
Swiss Life Asset Managers / Gelsenwasser / INNEXIS (Asset / Business Carve-Out) · Industrial Site Infrastructure & Services · Austria
Swiss Life- and Gelsenwasser-backed INNEXIS agreed to acquire selected infrastructure facilities, assets and real estate-related services at the Kundl Life Science Campus from Novartis, with 30 employees transferring. The acquired perimeter spans approximately 264,000 square metres and more than 100 buildings; Novartis will retain the research and production facilities.
Source
 
Milestone Environmental | I Squared Capital
I Squared Capital / SK Capital (Buyout) · Environmental Services · United States
I Squared Capital's flagship fund agreed to acquire Milestone Environmental from SK Capital. The platform owns and operates 15 fully permitted disposal facilities across major U.S. energy basins and manages approximately one billion gallons of waste annually across the energy and industrial sectors.
Source
Digital 2 Deals
Sabey Data Center Properties | Ares Management
Ares Management (Minority Equity Investment) · Data Centers · United States
Ares Secondaries funds made a minority equity investment in Sabey Data Center Properties. The U.S. platform spans six energized campuses with approximately 251 MW of operating capacity and more than four million square feet of mission-critical space.
Source
 
Northern Fiber Holding | iCON Infrastructure
iCON Infrastructure / Strategic Fiber Networks (Portfolio Company Acquisition) · Fiber Networks · Germany
iCON-backed Strategic Fiber Networks agreed to acquire Northern Fiber Holding in a share deal. Operating through the Lünecom and sewikom brands, Northern Fiber manages owned and leased networks serving rural and semi-urban communities across three German states.
Source
Transportation 2 Deals
D-Marin | InfraVia Capital Partners
InfraVia Capital Partners / CVC (Buyout / Sale) · Marina Infrastructure · Mediterranean / EMEA
InfraVia European Fund VI agreed to acquire D-Marin from CVC alongside management. The premium-marina platform operates and develops 28 marinas with more than 14,300 berths across nine countries.
Source
 
Helen EV Charging Business | CVC DIF
CVC DIF / Plugit / Helen (Portfolio Company Acquisition) · EV Charging · Finland
CVC DIF-backed Plugit acquired Helen’s electric-vehicle charging business. The network includes 199 public charging stations and 798 charge points, with more than 55,000 registered users transferring to Plugit’s platform.
Source
Utilities 1 Deal
Dresser Utility Solutions | Blackstone / First Reserve
Blackstone Energy Transition Partners / First Reserve (Buyout / Sale) · Utility Infrastructure Technology · United States
Funds managed by Blackstone Energy Transition Partners agreed to acquire Dresser Utility Solutions from First Reserve. The approximately 850-employee business supplies measurement, instrumentation and control products for natural-gas and water infrastructure globally; terms were not disclosed.
Source
Midstream 1 Deal
MidOcean Energy | EIG
EIG / Private Department (Primary Capital Raise / Minority Investment) · LNG · North America / Global
The Private Department of Sheikh Mohammed bin Khalid Al Nahyan committed US$1.13 billion to MidOcean Energy, the global LNG company formed and managed by EIG. The investment expands MidOcean’s institutional shareholder base and supports the platform’s LNG growth strategy.
