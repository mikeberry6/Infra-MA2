Weekly Briefing – Infrastructure Sponsor M&A – 9 deals across Power & ET, Midstream, Digital, Transportation, Utilities, and Social Infra – June 13–19, 2026
GUGGENHEIM
INFRASTRUCTURE COVERAGE & ADVISORY
Infrastructure Sponsor M&A Activity
Weekly Briefing • June 13 – June 19, 2026
KEY THEMES
Activity was led by Power & ET with 3 deals, while Midstream followed with 2 deals. Capital rotation remained visible in operating power assets, including Equitix’s Beatrice offshore wind stake purchase, Mubadala’s Greenlink interconnector investment, and I Squared’s ALTERRA co-investment into Peru’s Inkia Energy.
Direct deployment was strongest in scaled platforms and operating infrastructure: KKR committed $1.4bn to Altavair, CPP Investments committed up to C$1bn to CtrlS, Sixth Street invested $600mm in Pinnacle Gas Services, and TPG Transition Infrastructure signed two Southeastern U.S. waste platform acquisitions.
Power & ET 3 Deals
Beatrice Offshore Wind Farm | Equitix / InfraRed Capital Partners
Equitix / InfraRed Capital Partners (Minority Stake Acquisition / Divestiture) · Offshore Wind · United Kingdom
The Renewables Infrastructure Group received a binding offer to sell its full 17.5% stake in the 588 MW Beatrice offshore wind farm to funds managed by Equitix for approximately £155mm. The transaction recycles capital from a mature Scottish offshore wind asset and is expected to reduce TRIG’s revolving credit facility drawings.

Greenlink Interconnector | Mubadala
Mubadala (Minority Stake) · Power Transmission · United Kingdom / Ireland
Mubadala acquired US$200mm of Equitix’s stake in Greenlink, the 504 MW subsea electricity interconnector linking Great Britain and Ireland. The regulated HVDC asset spans approximately 190 km and supports cross-border power flows, renewables integration, and system flexibility.
Source
 
Inkia Energy | I Squared Capital
I Squared Capital (Co-Investment) · Power Generation · Peru
ALTERRA entered a co-investment in Inkia Energy alongside I Squared Capital through an I Squared-managed continuation vehicle. Inkia is Peru’s largest independent power producer, operating 2.6 GW of generation capacity and supporting a 4 GW renewables pipeline, including approximately 1 GW of near-term solar and wind projects.
Source
Midstream 2 Deals
KAPS Pipeline System | Stonepeak
Stonepeak (Divestiture / Exit) · NGL Pipeline · Canada
Keyera acquired Stonepeak’s remaining 50% non-operating interest in the KAPS Pipeline System for C$1.215bn. KAPS is a natural gas liquids pipeline system connecting Montney and Duvernay production to downstream markets, and Keyera now owns 100% of the asset.
Source
 
Pinnacle Gas Services | Sixth Street
Sixth Street (Minority Stake) · Natural Gas Gathering & Treating · United States
Sixth Street invested US$600mm for a 27% minority equity interest in Comstock Resources’ midstream subsidiary Pinnacle Gas Services. Pinnacle owns and operates natural gas gathering and treating systems supporting Comstock’s Western Haynesville development.
Source
Digital 1 Deal
CtrlS Datacenters | CPP Investments
CPP Investments (Minority Stake / Joint Venture) · Data Centers · India
CPP Investments committed up to C$1bn to CtrlS Datacenters, including INR 40bn, or approximately C$588mm, to acquire an 8.2% stake in the company. The partnership also includes a 48% CPP Investments interest in a new platform to develop hyperscale data center campuses across India.
Source
Transportation 1 Deal
Altavair Aircraft Leasing Portfolio | KKR
KKR (Growth Investment / Platform Expansion) · Aircraft Leasing · Global / United States
KKR committed US$1.4bn of equity to continue expanding its global leased commercial aircraft portfolio with Altavair. The new commitment builds on two prior aircraft leasing portfolios and is expected to be funded primarily through KKR’s Infrastructure and Asset-Based Finance strategies.
Source
Utilities 1 Deal
ThamesWey Central Milton Keynes | Ancala Partners
Ancala Partners (Portfolio Company Acquisition) · District Heating / Electricity Network · United Kingdom
Ancala-backed Leep Utilities acquired ThamesWey Central Milton Keynes from ThamesWey Energy. TCMK provides district heating and private-wire electricity network services to domestic and business customers in central Milton Keynes, including mixed-use developments and office assets.
Source
Social Infra 1 Deal
Waste Eliminator / Liberty Waste Solutions | TPG
TPG (Buyout / Platform Acquisition) · Waste Infrastructure · United States
TPG signed definitive agreements to acquire Waste Eliminator and Liberty Waste Solutions from Allied Industrial Partners through TPG Transition Infrastructure. The combination is expected to serve commercial, industrial, municipal, and residential customers across the Southeastern U.S. with 14 permitted facilities and more than 500 fleet vehicles.
