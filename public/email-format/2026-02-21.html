Infrastructure Sponsor M&A Activity
Weekly Briefing • February 21–27, 2026
KEY THEMES
Fifteen deals were reported, headlined by over $5bn in digital infrastructure M&A as CPP Investments and Equinix jointly agreed to acquire Nordic data center operator atNorth for $4.0bn and Brookfield launched its $1.3bn Radiant AI platform through Ori Industries. Macquarie Asset Management was the week’s most active seller, announcing three divestitures anchored by the $7.0bn sale of Macquarie AirFinance. Power & ET led deal count with six transactions focused on scaling distributed and renewable energy platforms, while the digital-power convergence theme continued as EQT-backed Scale Microgrids acquired data center power specialist Reload.
Power & ET 6 Deals
Reload
Scale Microgrids (Bolt-On) · Distributed Energy · US
Scale Microgrids, backed by EQT Transition Infrastructure, acquired Reload, a Chicago-based platform that sites, designs, and permits gigawatt-scale data center campuses paired with rapidly deployable on-site power infrastructure. The acquisition expands Scale’s growing data center business and is supported by a material capital commitment from EQT to deploy multi-gigawatts of capacity for hyperscale data center growth.
Source
 
Cleanwatts
DWS Infrastructure (Buyout) · Distributed Energy · Portugal
An infrastructure fund managed by DWS acquired 100% of Portuguese renewable energy communities platform Cleanwatts from Verdane. As part of the transaction, DWS committed approximately €150mm by 2030 to scale Cleanwatts’ decentralized solar and energy communities model, which provides commercial and residential users with shared solar generation with no upfront capital costs.
Source
 
Andion CH4 Renewables
Equitix (Growth Equity) · Energy Transition · Europe
Equitix led a considerable equity contribution into Andion CH4 Renewables, a European biogas developer, owner, and operator, alongside a €67mm private credit facility provided by Goldman Sachs Alternatives. The financing will accelerate the development of Andion’s short-term pipeline of biomethane projects across Italy and the Nordic region.
Source
 
Cella Dati Biomethane Plant
Andion CH4 Renewables (Bolt-On) · Energy Transition · Italy
Andion CH4 Renewables, a European biomethane platform backed by Equitix’s Next Generation Fund, announced a binding agreement to acquire 100% of the share capital of the Cella Dati biomethane plant from IREN and Santini Agricoltura Rinnovabile. The acquisition adds immediate operating capacity to Andion’s expanding footprint in Italy.
Source
 
HyCC (Hydrogen Chemistry Company)
Macquarie Asset Management (Divestiture) · Energy Transition · Europe
Macquarie Asset Management and Nobian agreed to sell their joint venture equity interest in HyCC, a large-scale green hydrogen developer, to Dutch e-fuels firm Power2X. Founded in 2021, HyCC holds a 1 GW development pipeline of green hydrogen projects across the Netherlands and Germany, including the 20 MW Djewels electrolyser project in Delfzijl.
Source
 
83MW Indian Solar Energy Projects
Digital Edge (Minority Stake) · Renewables · India
Digital Edge, an APAC data center platform backed by Stonepeak, acquired a substantial minority equity stake in an 83 MW solar project portfolio from Hexa Climate Solutions. The direct investment into the renewable generation assets ensures captive user status under Indian regulations to power Digital Edge’s BOM data center campus.
Source
Digital 5 Deals
atNorth
CPP Investments / Partners Group / Equinix (Buyout & Reinvestment) · Data Centers · Europe
CPP Investments and Equinix signed a definitive joint agreement to acquire a controlling 60% equity stake in atNorth from Partners Group for a $4.0bn enterprise value. CPP Investments will commit approximately $1.6bn to the transaction, while Equinix takes 40%. Concurrently, Partners Group will reinvest for up to a 10% minority equity stake in the high-density, pan-Nordic colocation platform. The deal includes a provisionally agreed $4.2bn financing package.
Source
 
Ori Industries
Brookfield Asset Management (Platform Acquisition / Merger) · AI Infrastructure · UK
Brookfield Asset Management officially launched Radiant, a vertically integrated AI infrastructure company, through the acquisition and merger of UK-based distributed AI cloud provider Ori Industries. Valued at $1.3bn post-merger, Radiant serves as the first compute deployment vehicle for Brookfield’s $10bn AI Infrastructure Fund (BAIIF). Built on the NVIDIA DSX reference design, Radiant will deliver utility-scale, purpose-built “AI factories” under long-term contracts.
Source
 
Sandy Farms & Eternal Rings Data Centers
Harrison Street (Divestiture) · Data Centers · US
Harrison Street sold two powered-shell data centers in the Baltimore, Maryland metro area to GI Partners for $221.9mm. The transaction includes the 109,455-square-foot Sandy Farms facility in Severn and the 110,336-square-foot Eternal Rings facility in Laurel. The acquisition by GI Partners was supported by a $113.4mm acquisition loan from Capital One Bank.
Source
 
Skellefteå Data Center Site
EdgeConneX (Bolt-On) · Data Centers · Sweden
EQT-backed EdgeConneX agreed to acquire a data center development site in Skellefteå, Sweden, from battery manufacturer Lyten. Co-located at the newly formed Lyten Industrial Hub at the former Northvolt Ett battery plant, the EdgeConneX campus has the potential to scale up to 1 GW of capacity. The facility will be primarily powered by local renewable hydroelectricity to support intensive AI and cloud computing workloads.
Source
 
Digital Sense
11:11 Systems (Bolt-On) · Cloud & Managed Services · Australia
Tiger Infrastructure Partners-backed 11:11 Systems acquired Digital Sense, a Brisbane-based cloud and managed services provider, from Aussie Broadband. The carve-out represents 11:11 Systems’ seventh acquisition of a former VMware Cloud Service Provider and expands its sovereign cloud capabilities and footprint in the Australian and wider APAC markets.
Source
Transportation 3 Deals
Macquarie AirFinance
Macquarie Asset Management (Divestiture) · Aviation Leasing · Global
Macquarie Asset Management (MAM) announced the sale of its 50% stake in Macquarie AirFinance (MAF) to Dubai Aerospace Enterprise (DAE) as part of an all-cash transaction valuing the lessor at a $7.0bn enterprise value. The seller consortium also included PGGM Infrastructure Fund and the Australian Retirement Trust. The acquisition adds 191 airline customers and pushes DAE’s combined pro forma fleet to 1,029 aircraft.
Source
 
Lower Lakes Towing & 6 Canadian-flagged Lakers
Mainstay Maritime (Divestiture) · Maritime Logistics · Canada
Mainstay Maritime, an Oaktree Transportation Infrastructure Investing Group portfolio company formerly known as Rand Logistics, agreed to sell its Canadian operating companies, including Lower Lakes Towing, to Algoma Central Corporation. The transaction includes six Canadian-flagged Laker vessels with capacities ranging up to 33,824 tons. The divestiture allows Mainstay Maritime to focus exclusively on its US-flagged, Jones Act-compliant fleet.
Source
 
321 Precision Conversions
ATSG (Divestiture) · Aviation Services · US
Air Transport Services Group (ATSG), an aviation logistics portfolio company recently taken private by Stonepeak, sold its 49% minority stake in 321 Precision Conversions to its joint venture partner, Erickson Group Ltd. The JV was formed in 2017 to engineer passenger-to-freighter conversions for Airbus A321 aircraft. The divestiture gives Erickson full ownership of the business as ATSG refocuses on core aircraft leasing operations.
Source
Waste & ES 1 Deal
DTG Recycle
Macquarie Infrastructure Partners (Divestiture) · Waste Management · US
Macquarie Infrastructure Partners exited its investment in DTG Recycle, Washington State’s largest independent vertically integrated construction and demolition (C&D) waste recycling business. Founder Dan Guimont repurchased the core operations, while select Pierce County assets, including a C&D recycling facility in Tacoma, were divested to Waste Connections.
