Infrastructure Sponsor M&A Activity
Weekly Briefing • April 25 – May 1, 2026
KEY THEMES
Direct deployment was robust this week, led by large utility take-privates and European energy transition deals. Stonepeak and Bernhard teamed up to acquire Cleco from Macquarie, BCI, and Manulife, while Blackstone committed €2bn to Eurowind and GIP combined Saavi with Grupo México’s power portfolio.
Capital rotation was also in effect. Brookfield monetized non-core hydro and liquid storage assets, KKR sold Axius Water, and Ares bought Blackstone’s 32.4% Rover Pipeline stake in a notable midstream sponsor-to-sponsor trade.
Power & ET 7 Deals
Saavi Energía & Grupo México Power Assets | GIP
GIP (Joint Venture) · Power Generation · Mexico
Global Infrastructure Partners, part of BlackRock, entered into a definitive agreement to combine its Saavi Energía portfolio company with Grupo México’s power generation assets. The resulting platform will have 4,510 MW of combined output and an approximately 5,000 MW project pipeline, with Grupo México owning 70% and GIP retaining a 30% minority stake.
Source
 
Eurowind Energy | Blackstone
Blackstone (Growth Equity) · Renewable Energy · Europe
Funds managed by Blackstone Infrastructure entered into a definitive agreement to invest up to €2bn in Eurowind Energy, a pan-European renewables developer and independent power producer. The perpetual capital will support Eurowind’s onshore wind, solar, battery storage, and biogas development activity across 16 European markets.
Source
 
Non-core U.S. Hydro Assets | Brookfield
Brookfield (Divestiture) · Hydroelectric Power · US
Brookfield Renewable agreed to sell its remaining 50% interest in a portfolio of non-core U.S. hydroelectric assets, fully monetizing the investment after last year’s initial 50% stake sale at the same valuation. Total proceeds upon closing are expected to be approximately $1bn, including roughly $500mm net to Brookfield Renewable.
Source
 
Nala Renewables | IFM
IFM (Buyout) · Renewable Energy · Europe
Funds managed by IFM Investors agreed to acquire the remaining 50% equity stake in London-based Nala Renewables from Trafigura, bringing IFM to full ownership. Nala manages a multi-technology portfolio of more than 2 GW across solar PV, onshore wind, and battery energy storage systems in strategic European markets.
Source
 
Perigus Energy | CIP
CIP (Platform Acquisition) · Renewable Energy · Europe
Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners completed its acquisition of Ørsted’s European onshore business through CI V and launched the platform as Perigus Energy. The company operates across Ireland, Germany, the UK, and Spain with 826 MW of installed and under-construction capacity and a multi-GW pipeline spanning wind, solar, and battery storage.
Source
 
Oriden | I Squared
I Squared (Platform Acquisition) · Renewable Energy · US
I Squared’s Energy Transition Fund acquired Oriden, a Pittsburgh-based renewable energy developer, from Mitsubishi Power Americas. Oriden has a 5+ GW solar PV and battery storage pipeline concentrated in MISO and PJM, and I Squared plans to invest approximately $300mm to scale the platform into a next-generation IPP.
Source
 
104 MW Community Solar Portfolio | Generate
Generate (Joint Venture) · Community Solar · US
Generate Capital and Monarch Private Capital closed a joint equity investment in a 104 MW community solar portfolio spanning more than 15 projects. The projects are expected to commence operations in the second and third quarters of 2026 and generate approximately $200mm of investment tax credits while expanding local clean power supply.
Source
Utilities 4 Deals
Cleco Group | Stonepeak & Bernhard
Stonepeak & Bernhard (Buyout) · Electric Utilities · US
Stonepeak and Bernhard Capital Partners agreed to acquire Cleco Group from a consortium comprising Macquarie Asset Management, BCI, and Manulife Investment Management. Cleco is a regulated electric utility serving approximately 298,000 customers in Louisiana, with eight generating units totaling 2,676 MW of capacity.
Source
 
Axius Water | KKR
KKR (Divestiture) · Water Utilities · US
CRH agreed to acquire U.S.-based water treatment platform Axius Water from KKR for $700mm. KKR’s Global Impact Fund originally co-established the platform, which provides water quality and nutrient removal infrastructure, and the sale advances CRH’s expansion as a North American water infrastructure provider.
Source
 
UGI PA Electric Utility | Argo
Argo (Carve-out) · Electric Utilities · US
Argo Infrastructure Partners reached a definitive agreement to acquire UGI Utilities’ Electric Division for approximately $470mm. The regulated electric utility operates roughly 2,700 miles of transmission and distribution lines and 14 substations in Pennsylvania’s Luzerne and Wyoming counties, representing Argo’s fourth utility investment in the state.
Source
 
Seven Brazilian Transmission Assets | GIC
GIC (Minority Stake) · Power Transmission · Brazil
GIC and Neoenergia expanded their Brazilian transmission partnership through a R$2.4bn transaction involving a 49% stake in seven transmission assets. The agreement strengthens the platform’s regulated transmission footprint, which is expected to include 16 assets totaling approximately 6,710 km of lines after completion.
Source
Digital 2 Deals
Elea Data Centers | I Squared
I Squared (Platform Acquisition) · Data Centers · Brazil
I Squared agreed to acquire Elea Data Centers, one of Brazil’s largest carrier-neutral data center platforms. Elea operates nine interconnected campuses across key Brazilian markets with more than 300 MW of powered land and over 1 GW in development, requiring more than US$10bn of capital to fully deliver.
Source
 
i3 Broadband | Wren House
Wren House (Joint Venture) · Fiber/Broadband · US
T-Mobile entered into a definitive agreement to form a 50/50 joint venture with Wren House to acquire i3 Broadband, with T-Mobile expected to invest approximately $700mm for its 50% equity interest. i3 Broadband is a fiber-to-the-premises operator serving customers across Illinois, Missouri, and Rhode Island.
Source
Midstream 2 Deals
Rover Pipeline | Blackstone & Ares
Ares (Minority Stake) & Blackstone (Divestiture) · Midstream · US
Ares Infrastructure Opportunities acquired a 32.4% minority equity stake in the Rover Pipeline from funds managed by Blackstone Energy Transition Partners. The 700-mile transmission pipeline provides 3.425 Bcf/d of Appalachian Basin takeaway capacity to Midwest demand centers and remains substantially contracted under long-term agreements.
Source
 
Inter Terminals | Brookfield
Brookfield (Divestiture) · Liquid Storage · Europe
Brookfield Infrastructure disclosed a signed definitive agreement to sell its Scandinavian bulk liquid storage business, Inter Terminals. The Nordic platform operates terminal locations across Denmark and Sweden with access to transport links and transhipment services, continuing Brookfield’s capital recycling program.
Source
Transportation 2 Deals
TrueNoord | Arcus & Patria
Arcus (Buyout) & Patria (Divestiture) · Aviation Leasing · Global
Arcus Infrastructure Partners entered into definitive documentation to acquire an approximately 74% majority stake in specialist regional aircraft lessor TrueNoord. The transaction facilitates an exit for previous investors including Patria, while founding investor Freshstream will reinvest alongside Arcus to support future fleet growth.
Source
 
Briggs Equipment UK | IFM
IFM (Platform Acquisition) · Logistics & Equipment · UK
IFM Investors agreed to acquire 100% of Briggs Equipment UK from Sammons Enterprises. Briggs is one of the largest independent materials handling platforms in the UK and Ireland, with more than 40 depots, over 850 engineers, and a managed fleet of approximately 53,000 assets serving infrastructure and industrial customers.
Source
