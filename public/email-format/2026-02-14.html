Infrastructure Sponsor M&A Activity
Weekly Briefing • February 14–20, 2026
KEY THEMES
Nine infrastructure transactions were reported this week, headlined by Macquarie’s A$11.7bn take-private of Qube Holdings, Australia’s largest ports operator. Power and energy transition led with four deals, including Infratil’s follow-on in Contact Energy’s NZ$525mm equity raise and a $100mm Series D for Utility Global’s hydrogen platform. Digital contributed nexfibre’s £2bn acquisition of Netomnia and Commerz Real’s minority stake in ATC Europe’s 30,000-tower portfolio. Blackstone acquired Marguerite’s 10% stake in Fraport Greece for approximately €200mm. QIC’s A$80mm co-investment in the Kalfresh bioenergy facility was the sole waste and environmental services transaction. Midstream, social, and utility sectors were inactive.
Power & ET 4 Deals
Contact Energy
Infratil (Follow-On) · Integrated Energy · New Zealand
Infratil participated pro rata in Contact Energy’s NZ$525mm equity raise, announced alongside half-year results showing a 44% jump in net profit to NZ$205mm. Proceeds will fund a 200 MW battery at Glenbrook, a 150 MW Glorit solar farm joint venture with Lightsource bp, and pre-FID drilling for the Tauhara 2 geothermal project to explore upsizing capacity from 50 MW to 60–70 MW. Infratil holds a 14.3% stake in New Zealand’s second-largest power generator.
Source
 
Utility Global
Ara Partners / APG Asset Management (Growth Equity) · Energy Transition · US
Ara Partners and APG Asset Management co-led a $100mm first close of Utility Global’s Series D financing. The capital will fund global deployment of the company’s proprietary H2Gen technology, which converts water into clean hydrogen using industrial off-gases without electricity, targeting hard-to-abate sectors including steel, refining, and petrochemicals.
Source
 
Duffy BESS Project
DESRI (Preferred Equity) · BESS · US
DESRI provided preferred equity financing to Linea Energy, an EnCap Investments portfolio company, for the 235 MWac Duffy battery energy storage system in Matagorda County, Texas. The two-hour duration BESS will participate in the ERCOT wholesale energy and ancillary services market.
Source
 
One Power Company
Basalt Infrastructure / OnSite Partners (Bolt-On) · Distributed Energy · US
Basalt Infrastructure, acting through portfolio company OnSite Partners, acquired Ohio-based One Power Company in an add-on transaction. The deal broadens OnSite Partners’ behind-the-meter distributed generation portfolio and customer base while adding construction and operational expertise in energy infrastructure for commercial and industrial clients.
Source
Digital 2 Deals
Substantial Group (Netomnia)
nexfibre (Buyout) · Fiber/Broadband · UK
InfraVia, Liberty Global, and Telefónica, acting through their joint venture nexfibre, agreed to acquire Substantial Group from DigitalBridge, Advencap, and Soho Square Capital for an enterprise value of £2bn. Netomnia is the UK’s second-largest alternative fiber provider, currently passing approximately 3mm premises and expected to reach 3.4mm by transaction close in Q3 2026. The combination will create a scaled challenger to BT Openreach targeting approximately 8mm premises by end-2027.
Source
 
ATC Europe
Commerz Real (Minority Stake) · Telecom Towers · Europe
Commerz Real, in partnership with Allianz Global Investors, acquired a minority stake in ATC Europe from Allianz insurance companies on behalf of its newly launched Infravest infrastructure fund. ATC Europe owns more than 30,000 wireless communication towers across Germany, France, and Spain as the European arm of American Tower Corporation.
Source
Transportation 2 Deals
Qube Holdings
Macquarie Asset Management (Buyout) · Ports & Logistics · Australia
A Macquarie Asset Management–led consortium including UniSuper and Pontegadea entered a binding scheme implementation deed to acquire Qube Holdings at A$5.20 cash per share, implying an enterprise value of approximately A$11.7bn and a 28% premium to the undisturbed price. Qube is Australia’s largest ports and terminal infrastructure provider, operating across 29 ports with a 10,000-strong workforce.
Source
 
Fraport Greece
Blackstone Infrastructure (Minority Stake) · Airports · Greece
Blackstone Infrastructure acquired the Marguerite Fund’s 10% stake in Fraport Greece for a reported approximately €200mm. Fraport Greece operates 14 regional airports under a 40-year concession that began in April 2017, having handled over 12mm more passengers in 2025 than in the 2016 baseline year. Fraport AG retains a 73.4% controlling stake.
Source
Waste & ES 1 Deal
Kalfresh Bioenergy Facility
QIC / Wollemi Capital (Co-Investment) · Waste-to-Energy · Australia
QIC and Wollemi Capital co-invested A$80mm in the Kalfresh Bioenergy Facility, the centerpiece of a A$291mm agricultural-industrial precinct at Kalbar, Queensland. The facility will use anaerobic digestion to convert food waste and crop residues into renewable natural gas, producing enough energy to power up to 31,000 homes and abating up to 430,000 tonnes of CO₂ annually. First energy production is scheduled for mid-2027.
