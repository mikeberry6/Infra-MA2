Infrastructure Sponsor M&A Activity
Weekly Briefing • March 14 – March 20, 2026
KEY THEMES
Quiet week for sponsor infrastructure M&A with just 10 qualifying transactions and no headline platform buyouts. The largest deal was Ares’s €1bn+ Plenitude capital increase, followed by KKR’s $310mm e-bus investment in India, with remaining activity skewing toward co-investments, minority stakes, and bolt-ons.
Transportation punched above its weight at 40% of deal flow, including KKR’s e-bus platform in India, CBRE’s entry into UK rolling stock via Porterbrook, I Squared’s highway consolidation ahead of Cube Highways’ InvIT conversion, and Zenōbē’s US e-truck bolt-on.
Power & ET 5 Deals
Plenitude (Eni) | Ares Management
Ares Management (Growth Equity) · Renewables · Italy
Eni announced a ~€1.5bn capital increase for Plenitude, its renewables and retail energy unit, with at least €1bn from Ares (€10.75bn pre-money equity valuation). The transaction establishes joint control between Eni (~65%) and Ares, deconsolidating Plenitude.
Source
 
Bayonne Energy Center | Morgan Stanley Infrastructure Partners
Morgan Stanley Infrastructure Partners (Divestiture) · Thermal Power · US
MSIP agreed to sell its stake in Thermal Bayonne Holdings, a 660 MW dual-fuel peaking facility in Bayonne, NJ, to Jupiter Energy Investor. The plant serves NYC via a dedicated subsea cable. Jefferies served as financial advisor.
Source
 
Spanish Hybridisation Platform | Equitix
Equitix / COFIDES (Co-Investment) · Renewables · Spain
COFIDES committed up to €150mm through Spain’s FOCO co-investment fund to join Equitix’s renewable hybridisation platform, co-owned with Australia’s TCorp. The platform targets 1.4 GW of wind, solar, and storage by 2030, with 330 MW operational or under construction.
Source
 
174 MW Wind Portfolio (France) | Axium Infrastructure Europe
Axium Infrastructure Europe (Minority Stake) · Wind · France
Axium Infrastructure Europe, a Canadian infrastructure fund, acquired a 49.9% interest in a 174 MW operational wind portfolio from la Banque des Territoires, with ENGIE Green retaining 50.1%. The 22-project portfolio spans three French regions with revenues under government FiT and CfD contracts.
Source
 
48.4 MW Community Solar Portfolio (New Mexico) | Brookfield
Brookfield / Standard Solar (Bolt-On) · Solar · US
Standard Solar, a Brookfield portfolio company, acquired a 48.4 MW, eight-project community solar portfolio in New Mexico from Pluma Construction and ForeFront Power — among the first projects delivered under the state’s Community Solar Program.
Source
Transportation 4 Deals
Porterbrook | CBRE Investment Management
CBRE Investment Management (Minority Stake) · Rolling Stock · UK
CBRE IM’s Private Infrastructure division agreed to acquire a 10% stake in Porterbrook, the UK’s second-largest rolling stock lessor with ~4,300 rail vehicles (~25% of the national fleet).
Source
 
Cube Highways Trust (4 Highway Assets) | I Squared Capital
I Squared Capital / Cube Highways (Asset Acquisition) · Highways · India
Cube Highways Trust, backed by I Squared Capital, executed a binding commitment to acquire four highway entities from its sponsor via equity swap. The Trust manages 27 road assets spanning 2,000+ km across 12 Indian states and filed with SEBI for public InvIT conversion.
Source
 
PMI Electro / Allfleet | KKR
KKR (Growth Equity) · EV Fleet · India
KKR agreed to invest up to $310mm for a majority stake in Allfleet and a minority stake in PMI Electro Mobility, India’s leading e-bus manufacturer (2,900+ buses, 31 cities). Capital will fund 5,000+ e-buses under long-term concessions.
Source
 
Revolv | KKR
KKR / Zenōbē (Bolt-On) · EV Fleet · US
Zenōbē (KKR, Infracapital, Mubadala) acquired California-based Revolv to enter the US commercial e-truck market, adding 13 operational sites and 100+ electric trucks to its 3,400+ global vehicle fleet.
Source
Digital 1 Deal
NorthC Datacenters | APG Asset Management
APG Asset Management (Co-Investment) · Data Centers · Netherlands
APG Asset Management, the Dutch pension investor, co-invested in NorthC Datacenters alongside Antin Infrastructure Partners’ platform buyout from DWS (Antin Fund V). NorthC operates 25 colocation data centers across the Netherlands, Germany, and Switzerland with 140+ MW of secured grid capacity.
