Infrastructure Sponsor M&A Activity
Weekly Briefing • May 16 – May 22, 2026
KEY THEMES
Energy transition dominated volume, with 7 of 15 deals spanning renewable power, hydro/storage, biomethane, green hydrogen and batteries, including Patria / Omnia’s $2bn renewable-power supply structure, CIP / BII’s $300mm North Star launch, PSP’s FirstLight sale and GSAM / GridStor’s Birdseye acquisition.
North American direct deployment cut across concessions, storage and operating services: Stonepeak pursued Chicago parking-meter rights, GSAM / GridStor bought the Birdseye Colorado battery project and Tallvine added Lind Marine, while PSP’s FirstLight exit moved a 1.4 GW U.S. clean-power portfolio to Hull Street Energy.
Power & ET7 Deals
Casa dos Ventos Renewable-Power Assets | Omnia / Patria
Patria / Omnia (Joint Venture) · Renewable Power / Data Center Energy · Brazil
Omnia, Patria's data center platform, signed a $2bn, 20-year renewable energy supply agreement with Casa dos Ventos to power its Pecem data center for ByteDance. The self-production structure gives Omnia a stake in wind assets including the 630 MW Ibiapaba complex, tying renewable generation to a large hyperscale development.
Source
 
North Star Renewable Energy Platform | CIP / BII
CIP (Platform Launch) · Solar / Wind / Storage · India
CIP and British International Investment launched North Star, a $300mm India renewables platform with each party committing up to $150mm. The platform will invest in solar, wind, hybrid renewable and storage projects, addressing development-capital bottlenecks in India's clean-power buildout.
Source
 
FirstLight U.S. Clean-Power Portfolio | PSP Investments
PSP Investments (Sale) · Hydro / Storage / Renewables · United States
PSP agreed to sell FirstLight's U.S. operations to Hull Street Energy. The portfolio includes about 1.4 GW of hydroelectric generation, energy storage and renewable assets in Massachusetts, Connecticut and Pennsylvania, while PSP retains FirstLight's Canadian platform.
Source
 
Birdseye Battery Storage Project | GridStor / GSAM
GSAM / GridStor (Portfolio Company Acquisition) · Battery Storage · United States
GridStor acquired the 199 MW / 796 MWh Birdseye battery energy storage project in Adams County, Colorado from Accelergen. Backed by Goldman Sachs Asset Management, GridStor expects construction as early as 2027 and commercial operations by the end of 2028.
Source
 
132 MW Saxony Onshore Wind Portfolio | Ardian
Ardian (Buyout) · Onshore Wind · Germany
Ardian Clean Energy Evergreen Fund acquired a greenfield onshore wind project portfolio in Saxony totaling 132 MW once constructed. The deal is ACEEF's first German investment and anchors a broader renewables platform in Europe's largest renewable-energy market.
Source
 
Barrow Green Hydrogen Project | Schroders Greencoat / GHECO
Schroders Greencoat (Joint Venture) · Green Hydrogen · United Kingdom
Schroders Greencoat and Carlton Power reached FID through GHECO for the 30 MW Barrow Green Hydrogen Project in Cumbria. The project will use Plug electrolyzers to supply industrial green hydrogen to Kimberly-Clark and is one of the first UK renewable hydrogen projects to reach FID.
Source
 
APF Energy | Schroders Greencoat
Schroders Greencoat (Buyout) · Biomethane · Netherlands
Schroders Greencoat acquired 100% of APF Energy from SWEN Capital Partners' impact strategy and APF BV. APF is a Dutch biomethane platform producing renewable gas from agricultural manure and food by-products, with assets positioned for the renewable-molecules market.
Source
Transportation3 Deals
Chicago Parking Meters LLC | Stonepeak
Stonepeak (Buyout) · Parking Concession · United States
Stonepeak agreed to acquire ownership interests tied to Chicago Parking Meters LLC, which operates the city's 75-year municipal parking-meter concession. The transfer is subject to Chicago City Council approval and would shift control of a long-dated urban mobility revenue asset from the existing ownership group.
Source
 
Lind Marine | Tallvine Partners
Tallvine (Buyout) · Marine Infrastructure Services · United States
Tallvine acquired Lind Marine, expanding its North American marine infrastructure platform to the West Coast following its Donjon Marine acquisition. Lind provides dredging, towing, salvage, shipyard, environmental remediation and harbor and waterway services from its Mare Island base in the San Francisco Bay.
Source
 
Equans Infra & Mobility / Velian | DigitalBridge / InfraBridge / Aberdeen
DigitalBridge / InfraBridge / Aberdeen (Buyout) · EV Charging / E-Mobility · Netherlands
A DigitalBridge-controlled vehicle and Aberdeen Investments acquired Equans' Dutch asset-based e-mobility activities, which will operate as Velian. The platform designs, finances, builds and operates public and private EV charging infrastructure for government, real estate and logistics customers in the Netherlands.
Source
Utilities2 Deals
Cube Grid | I Squared Capital
I Squared (Platform Launch) · Power Transmission · India
I Squared launched Cube Grid, a power transmission platform in India, with plans to deploy up to $1bn of equity capital. The platform has signed definitive agreements for seed assets totaling more than 1,450 circuit kilometers of transmission lines alongside Dineshchandra Group.
Source
 
Kalmar Energi 50% Stake | Nordion Energi / Igneo
Igneo / Nordion Energi (Buyout) · Electricity Grids / District Heating · Sweden
Nordion Energi, owned by EDIF II and managed by Igneo, signed an agreement to acquire E.ON Sverige's 50% stake in Kalmar Energi. The integrated utility operates across electricity grids, district heating and fiber, with Kalmar Municipality continuing as joint owner.
Source
Digital1 Deal
Seraya Partners Fund I Interest | GenZero
GenZero / Temasek (Secondaries) · Energy Transition / Digital Infrastructure · Asia-Pacific
GenZero acquired a secondary interest in Seraya Partners Fund I from AIIB, gaining exposure to an Asia-focused infrastructure fund targeting energy transition and digital infrastructure. The portfolio includes Cyan Renewables, a marine energy-transition operator, and Empyrion Digital, a pan-Asia low-carbon data center platform.
Source
Midstream1 Deal
Exolum | OMERS Infrastructure
OMERS Infrastructure (Sale) · Energy Logistics · Europe
OMERS agreed to sell its roughly 25% stake in Exolum, with Stoneshield Capital acquiring 15% and another global investor buying the remaining 10%. Exolum operates refined-products and aviation-fuel logistics infrastructure, including pipeline networks in Spain and the UK, 68 storage terminals and service to more than 48 airports.
Source
Social Infra1 Deal
Information Services Corporation | Plenary Americas / La Caisse
La Caisse / Plenary Americas (Buyout) · Public Registry Services · Canada
Plenary Americas signed an all-cash arrangement agreement to acquire Information Services Corporation for C$51.00 per share, valuing ISC at about C$1.2bn enterprise value. ISC provides registry and information management services for public data and records, including land and business registry functions.
Source
