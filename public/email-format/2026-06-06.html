Infrastructure Sponsor M&A Activity
Weekly Briefing • May 30 – June 5, 2026
KEY THEMES
Power & ET accounted for 2 of 2 deals, both tied to grid-scale storage and renewable-power development platforms. The week’s activity was concentrated in battery storage, with EIG / Fidra Energy adding one of the UK’s largest BESS development projects and Igneo Infrastructure Partners backing a U.S. hybrid solar-and-storage platform.
Direct North American deployment came through Igneo’s preferred equity investment in Pathway Power, which gives the manager exposure to a more than 1 GW development portfolio across SPP and MISO. In Europe, EIG-backed Fidra Energy continued to scale its UK storage pipeline with the 1,025 MW Enderby project acquisition from Innova.
Power & ET 2 Deals
Pathway Power | Igneo Infrastructure Partners
Igneo Infrastructure Partners (Preferred Equity Investment) · Renewable Power / Battery Storage · United States
Igneo Infrastructure Partners completed a preferred equity investment in Pathway Power, a U.S. utility-scale renewable and storage development platform. Pathway has assembled more than 1 GW of projects expected to begin construction in 2026-2027, plus approximately 1,000 MW of early-stage hybrid interconnection positions across SPP and MISO.

Enderby Battery Storage Project | EIG
EIG / Fidra Energy (Portfolio Company Acquisition) · Battery Storage · United Kingdom
Fidra Energy, an EIG-backed BESS platform, acquired the Enderby battery storage project from Innova for an undisclosed sum. The Leicestershire project is expected to deliver up to 1,025 MW once operational, has planning consent, and increases Fidra’s UK BESS pipeline to more than 4 GW.
