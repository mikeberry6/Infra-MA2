Infrastructure Sponsor M&A Activity
Weekly Briefing • May 30 – June 5, 2026
KEY THEMES
Power & ET was the most active sector with 5 of 9 deals, led by grid-scale storage and renewable-platform activity across the U.S., UK, and Uruguay.
U.S. activity centered on renewable power and digital infrastructure: Igneo Infrastructure Partners invested in Pathway Power, Infratil committed additional growth equity to Longroad Energy, and NOVA-backed DartPoints added a Lexington data center campus.
Power & ET 5 Deals
Pathway Power | Igneo Infrastructure Partners
Igneo Infrastructure Partners (Preferred Equity Investment) · Renewable Power / Battery Storage · United States
Igneo Infrastructure Partners completed a preferred equity investment in Pathway Power, a U.S. utility-scale renewable and storage development platform. Pathway has assembled more than 1 GW of projects expected to begin construction in 2026-2027, plus approximately 1,000 MW of early-stage hybrid interconnection positions across SPP and MISO.
Source
 
Enderby Battery Storage Project | EIG
EIG / Fidra Energy (Portfolio Company Acquisition) · Battery Storage · United Kingdom
Fidra Energy, an EIG-backed BESS platform, acquired the Enderby battery storage project from Innova for an undisclosed sum. The Leicestershire project is expected to deliver up to 1,025 MW once operational, has planning consent, and increases Fidra’s UK BESS pipeline to more than 4 GW.
Source
 
Uruguay Solar PV Portfolio | Ardian / GIP
Ardian / Global Infrastructure Partners (Acquisition / Divestiture) · Solar PV · Uruguay
Ardian Clean Energy Evergreen Fund acquired two operating solar PV plants in Uruguay from Global Infrastructure Partners with 76 MWp of combined capacity. The transaction marks Ardian’s entry into Uruguay and expands its Latin American renewable-power footprint.
Source
 
Stoneworthy Battery Storage Project | Equitix
Equitix / Eelpower Energy (Portfolio Company Acquisition) · Battery Storage · United Kingdom
Equitix-backed Eelpower Energy acquired the Stoneworthy battery energy storage project in Devon from RES. The project is a 50 MW / 100 MWh BESS asset and adds to Eelpower’s UK storage platform.
Source
 
Longroad Energy | Infratil
Infratil (Growth Equity Funding) · Renewable Power / Battery Storage · United States
Infratil agreed to provide additional growth equity funding to Longroad Energy, its U.S. renewable-energy platform focused on solar, wind, and battery storage development and operations. The commitment supports continued buildout of Longroad’s power-infrastructure pipeline.
Source
Social Infra 2 Deals
Estia Health | Stonepeak
Stonepeak (Majority Stake) · Residential Aged Care · Australia
A Stonepeak-led consortium agreed to acquire a majority stake in Estia Health from Bain Capital, extending Stonepeak’s Volarae Living social-infrastructure platform in Australia and New Zealand. Estia is one of Australia’s largest residential aged-care operators, with more than 90 homes across four states and approximately 9,000 residents.
Source
 
Finnish Social Infrastructure Portfolio | PATRIZIA
PATRIZIA / Kinland (Bolt-On Asset Acquisition) · Education / Care Properties · Finland
PATRIZIA-backed Kinland completed the acquisition of three Finnish social infrastructure properties from United Bankers’ UB Finnish Properties fund. The bolt-on expands Kinland’s Nordic portfolio of essential education and care assets.
Source
Digital 1 Deal
Lexington Data Center Campus | NOVA Infrastructure
NOVA Infrastructure / DartPoints (Portfolio Company Acquisition) · Data Centers · United States
NOVA-backed DartPoints acquired a Lexington, Kentucky data center campus to support AI, neo-cloud, hyperscale, and enterprise demand. The campus spans approximately 343,000 square feet on 29.5 acres, includes about 81,000 square feet of existing raised-floor data center space, and has a 20-30 MW initial path with longer-term expansion potential to 70 MW.
Source
Utilities 1 Deal
Naturgy Energy Group | CVC
CVC (Sale) · Integrated Gas / Power Utility · Spain
CVC sold its entire 13.8% stake in Naturgy Energy Group through an accelerated bookbuild and related derivative settlement. The exit covered a Spanish integrated gas and power utility stake worth around €4bn at prevailing market prices.
