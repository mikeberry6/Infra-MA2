Infrastructure Sponsor M&A Activity
Weekly Briefing • April 4 – April 10, 2026
KEY THEMES
Digital infrastructure drove the week’s activity, led by the Rowan Digital Infrastructure recapitalization and adjacent sponsor-backed expansion in both Los Angeles and the UK data center market.
Direct sponsor deployment spanned nuclear services (ECP), onshore wind (Equitix), wastewater upcycling (Ara), and data centers (Blackstone), while EQT broadened NFI’s investor base through a 30% minority stake sale.
Digital 3 Deals
Rowan Digital Infrastructure | Quinbrook / Blackstone
Blackstone (Minority Stake Acquisition) · Data Centers · US
Quinbrook announced a strategic recapitalization of Rowan Digital Infrastructure through the sale of a 49% significant minority stake to funds affiliated with Blackstone. The transaction monetizes part of Quinbrook’s ownership while retaining control, partnering with Blackstone to support Rowan’s next phase of growth and continued hyperscale data center expansion serving cloud and AI-driven demand.

DataBank / Goodman Los Angeles JV (32 MW) | Swiss Life AM / IMCO / AustralianSuper
DataBank / Goodman (Joint Venture) · Data Centers · US
DataBank and Goodman formed a 50/50 joint venture to develop a 32 MW AI-ready data center in Vernon, California. The arrangement advances sponsor-backed digital infrastructure development in a supply-constrained Los Angeles market and adds to DataBank’s broader U.S. expansion pipeline.
Source
 
Hemel Hempstead Data Center | DWS
DWS / Stellanor (Bolt-On) · Data Centers · UK
DWS, through portfolio company Stellanor, acquired a purpose-built data center in Hemel Hempstead from Imagination Technologies in a sale-and-service-back structure. The add-on expands Stellanor’s UK footprint while keeping the seller as a continuing service customer at the site.
Source
Power & ET 2 Deals
EnergySolutions | ECP
ECP (Buyout) · Nuclear Services · US
ECP agreed to acquire EnergySolutions in a full platform buyout, returning the nuclear services provider to infrastructure ownership. The deal adds exposure to essential decommissioning, waste management, and broader nuclear lifecycle services as electricity demand and energy security priorities continue to reshape the sector.
Source
 
Canales Sur Wind Project (120 MW) | Equitix
Equitix (Majority Stake Acquisition) · Onshore Wind · Spain
Equitix acquired a majority equity stake in the 120 MW Canales Sur onshore wind project in Spain, extending its partnership with Capital Energy. The deal adds another renewables asset to Equitix’s Iberian hybridisation strategy, with future solar and storage expansion expected alongside the wind platform.
Source
Social 1 Deal
Sedron Technologies | Ara Partners
Ara Partners (Growth Equity) · Wastewater / Resource Recovery · US
Ara committed up to $500mm to Sedron Technologies to scale infrastructure that converts wastewater and organic waste streams into reusable resources and clean electricity. The investment reflects continuing sponsor interest in circular infrastructure platforms with both environmental services and energy outputs.
Source
Transportation 1 Deal
Nordic Ferry Infrastructure | EQT
EQT (Minority Stake Sale) · Ferry / Maritime Transport · Nordics
EQT agreed to sell a 30% minority stake in Nordic Ferry Infrastructure to a consortium of long-term investors while retaining a 70% majority interest. The transaction broadens the platform’s investor base following its transformation into a pan-Nordic maritime transportation infrastructure business.
